My Mother's Life Lessons at 45

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up

Posted on January 01, 2017

At 20 something, I always feel a little old but not too wise. In a conversation I was having with my mother, I ended up asking her when wisdom “kicked in” for her. Instead of answering, she asked me to list down what I’ve learned from life so far. To my 12 life lessons, she added another 20 and this is what our list looks like:

1. Life isn’t fair, but it’s good.

2. You can both be happy and sad at the same time. Do not question either emotions.

3. When in doubt, just take the next small step.

4. Don’t take everyone so seriously. They don’t take you seriously.

5. What other people think of you is none of your business.

6. You don’t have to win every argument. Sometimes, just agree to disagree.

7. No one is in charge of your happiness except you.

8. Overprepare yourself for the stage, and then go with the flow.

9. You don’t get over jitters. You work through them.

10. Be eccentric now. Don’t wait for old age to wear neon.

11. Curl your hair, wear makeup, put on that fancy dress. Don’t save it for a special occasion. Today is special. (Guys, wear shirts more often, if you like shirts!)

12. Make peace with your past so it won’t mess with your present.

13. If a relationship has to be a secret, you shouldn’t be in it.

14. Love is fleeting. Don’t pretend you understand it.

15. Forgive everyone everything, but don’t forget.

16. Time can heal almost everything. Give it some time.

17. What doesn’t kill you really does make you stronger.

18. Frame every supposed disaster into: “In five years, will this even matter?”

19. Don’t compare your life to others’. You don’t know where they’ve come from or where they are heading.

20. If we all threw our problems in a pile with everyone else’s, we’d grab ours back.

21. All that truly matters in the end is that you loved.

22. Life is too short for long self-pity parties. Get busy living, or just die.

23. Always choose living over dying. You can get through anything. You can never be irreparably damaged.

24. No matter what is going on in your life, the world keeps spinning around.

25. Don’t inspect life. Show up and make the most of it now.

26. Get outside every day. Miracles are waiting to happen.

27. Your job won’t take care of you when you are sick.

28. A writer writes. If you want to become a writer, just write.

29. Get rid of anything that isn’t useful.

30. If you don’t ask for what you want, you don’t get it.

31. However good or bad a situation is, it will change.

32. The best is yet to come.
